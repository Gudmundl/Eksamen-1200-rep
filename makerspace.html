Oslomet Makerspace

OsloMet Makerspace is an open workshop at OsloMet where students can bring their own ideas and receive assistance with their projects. This creative space provides students with the opportunity to explore, create, and collaborate across disciplines. Here are some key points about Makerspace:

Modern facilities

This community-driven workshop provides students and staff at OsloMet with the opportunity to meet, collaborate, share knowledge, and create new things in innovative ways. The makerspace is a room filled with tools and cutting-edge technology that allows you to explore various creative fields

3D Printing

Makerspace has both simple FDM printers (suitable for most projects) and SLA printers (for parts with special properties like high thermal resistance) .

Programming stations

Programming Stations: Dive into coding and software development. Collaboration Areas: Work together on projects and exchange ideas.

Electronics and IoT

Electronics and IoT Makerspace provides a wide range of electrical components for electronics prototyping. Microcontrollers such as Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and Microbit are available for use. Sensors and actuators support IoT projects.

CNC Machining

Benchtop milling machines cater to larger projects. A 4-axis CNC machine and a smaller 3-axis CNC machine are dedicated to electronics and small-scale projects (excluding metalwork).
